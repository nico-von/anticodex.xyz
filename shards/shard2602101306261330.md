In a [[A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate|prisoner's dillema]], to [[Players in a 'prisoner's dilemma' can either 'defect' or 'cooperate'|'defect']] is the [[A 'dominant strategy' is when a player's best strategy never changes no matter what strategy the other players play|dominant strategy]] for both players, however, if both 'defect' they will do worse([[The outcome in a dominant strategy is that the player always gets the relative best payoff(guaranteed) of each situation and not the absolute best payoff(i.e. The highest reward)|the dominant strategy does not guarantee the best payoff]]) than they would if they both 'cooperate' instead
