Verb conjugations to indicate the doer of the action in Italian

Present:
When the doer is the speaker([['io' is the word for 'I' in Italian|'io']]):
1. [[Regular italian 'to {verb}' forms can be changed to an ending in '-o' to show that the speaker is the one doing the action]]

When the doer is the person the speaker is talking to([['Tu' is the word for saying and emphasising 'you'(informal) in Italian|'tu']]):
1. [[Regular Italian verbs in their 'to {verb}' form can be transformed to indicate that the doer is the person the speaker is talking to by replacing the'to {verb}' form ending with '-i']]

When the doer is the speaker himself and one or more other people considered together([['Noi' is for saying 'we' and adding emphasis to the doers referred to as 'we', often used with Italian verbs that end with '-iamo'|'noi']]):
1. [[The Italian ending '-iamo' indicates that the speaker refers to himself and one or more other people considered together, in other words, 'we']]
2. [[To have regular Italian verbs have their 'to {verb}' verb forms conjugated to refer to the speaker and one or more other people considered together, replace the verb form ending with '-iamo']]

When the doer is a third person singular or [[The third person singular conjugation is used for addressing someone formally in Italian|second person formal]] ([['lui' and 'lei' are more often used than other Italian words that gives emphasis to the doer of verbs because they help clarify who performs the action as the verb form alone could be ambiguous|'lui', 'lei']], [['Lei' is 'you'(formal) in Italian|'Lei']]):
1. [[In Italian, the regular '-are' verb form when conjugated to show that the action is done by a third person, i.e. 'he', 'she', or, 'it', its 'to {verb}' ending is replaced with '-a']]
2. [[In Italian, regular '-ere' and '-ire' verb forms when conjugated to show that the action is done by a third person, i.e. 'he', 'she', or, 'it', its 'to {verb}' ending is replaced with '-e']]

When the doer is a third person plural([['Loro' is 'they' and for emphasis of 'they' in Italian|'loro']]):
1. [[Regular '-ire' and '-ere' Italian verbs can be conjugated to indicate that the doer is a third person plural 'they' by changing their 'to {verb}' form ending to '-ono']]
2. [[Regular '-are' Italian verbs can be conjugated to indicate that the doer is a third person plural 'they' by changing their 'to {verb}' form ending to '-ano']]

When the doer is the second person plural([['voi' is the plural of 'tu' in Italian, and as such, it emphasises 'you'|'voi']]):
1. [[In Italian, the regular '-are' verb form when conjugated to show that the action is done by a plural second person, 'voi', its 'to {verb}' ending is replaced with '-ate']]
2. [[In Italian, the regular '-ire' verb form when conjugated to show that the action is done by a plural second person, 'voi', its 'to {verb}' ending is replaced with '-ite']]

About irregular verbs and conjugating them:
1. [[Not every Italian 'to {verb}' form ending can be altered by a single letter alone to mark the subject who does the action. Others are irregular.]]
2. [[Irregular verbs are prevalent in most commonly used verbs, especially in Italian]]
