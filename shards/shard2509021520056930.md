The plural of [['Il' is Italian for 'the' (for singular masculine words)|'il']] is 'i', ([[In Italian, 'Gli' is the article 'the' for plural words that are masculine and start with vowels|not used on all plural words, used only on masculine plural words that would not be used with 'gli']])

'Il' has [[A 'phonetic habit' of Italian is that most words tend to end in vowels.|no ending vowel]] so the 'l' is removed to get the [[To get the plural of some Italian words, replace the ending vowel with '-i', like how 'lo' becomes 'li'|'i']] ending for its plural instead.
