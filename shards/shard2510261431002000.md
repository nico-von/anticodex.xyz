List of possessive Italian words

Note on [[Italian words where the ending changes depending on the gender of what it describes|endings]]: [[Unlike English, where 'his' is used if the possessor is 'male' and 'her' if 'female', in Italian, the gender of the possessive word depends on the gender of the thing possessed, not of the possessor|Ending changes according to the gender of the thing possessed]]

1. [['mia' or 'mio' is the possessive 'my' in Italian]], [[The plural of the Italian possessive word 'mio' is 'miei']] 
2. [['tuo' is the Italian masculine word for 'your']], [['tua' is the Italian feminine word for 'your']], [[The plural of the Italian possessive 'tuo' is 'tuoi']] 
3. [['Suo' or 'Sua' is the possessive 'his' or 'her' in Italian]], [['suoi' and 'sue' are the plural forms of 'suo' and 'sua']] 
4. [['vostr...-o,-i,-a,-e' is 'your'(plural) in Italian]]
5. [['Nostro' is 'our' in Italian]]
6. [['loro' is the possessive 'their' in Italian]], [['loro' is also the plural form or the Italian possessive 'loro']]

Formal:
1. [['Suo'(capital S) is the formal form of 'tuo' in Italian]]
2. [['Suoi' is the possessive 'your'(plural formal) in Italian]]

Note on use: 
[[In Italian, to say that something possesses something else, the form 'the {possessive}' is generally used]]
Also, [[In Italian, possessive words are made plural when the thing possessed is plural]]
