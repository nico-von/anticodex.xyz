List of possessive Italian words

Note on [[Italian words where the ending changes depending on the gender of what it describes|endings]]: [[Unlike English, where 'his' is used if the possessor is 'male' and 'her' if 'female', in Italian, the gender of the possessive word depends on the gender of the thing possessed, not of the possessor|Ending changes according to the gender of the thing possessed]]

Singular:
1. [['mia' or 'mio' is the possessive 'my' in Italian]]
2. [['tuo' is the Italian masculine word for 'your']], [['tua' is the Italian feminine word for 'your']]
3. [['Suo' or 'Sua' is the possessive 'his' or 'her' in Italian]]
4. [['Nostro' is 'our' in Italian]]
5. [['loro' is the possessive 'their' in Italian]]

Plural([[In Italian, possessive words are made plural when the thing possessed is plural|when the thing possessed is plural]]):
1. [[The plural of the Italian possessive word 'mio' is 'miei']]
2. [[The plural of the Italian possessive 'tuo' is 'tuoi']]
3. [['suoi' and 'sue' are the plural forms of 'suo' and 'sua']]
4. [['loro' is also the plural form or the Italian possessive 'loro']]

Note on use: [[In Italian, to say that something possesses something else, the form 'the {possessive}' is generally used]]
