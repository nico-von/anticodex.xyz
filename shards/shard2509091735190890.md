A list of [[What directly receives the action of a verb is called a 'direct object'|'direct object']] Italian pronouns:
1. [['Mi' is 'myself' in Italian]]
2. [['you' in Italian is 'ti']]
3. [['vi' is the plural form of 'ti' in Italian]]
4. [['It' in Italian is 'lo'|'lo']] is the [[As 'la' is the Italian feminine for 'it', 'lo', is the Italian masculine|masculine form for 'it']]
5. [['La' is the feminine form of 'it' in Italian]]
6. [['lo' is also 'him' in Italian]]
7. [['la' is also 'her' in Italian]]
8. [[The plural of 'lo', or 'it', is 'li' in Italian. 'Li' means 'them']]
9. [['le' is the feminine direct object 'them' in Italian]]
10. [['ci' is 'us', 'each other', 'one another', 'ourselves' in Italian]]
11. [[In Italian, 'si' is used to refer to oneself(in the third person) when a subject does the action on themselves]]
