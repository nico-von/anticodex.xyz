Words ending in [[English words ending in '-ity' can be transformed to Italian by changing '-ity' to '-ità'|'-ità']] are feminine. 

(Indicated by their [[Italian nouns ending in 'a' are feminine|ending sound 'a']] as well as the use of [['La' is Italian for 'the' (for singular feminine words)|'la']] as their article(see [[Nouns ending in '-ità' retain their endings when transformed into their plural form, like 'le possibilità'|how 'le possibilità' was used as an example for the plural form of nouns ending in '-ità']]))
