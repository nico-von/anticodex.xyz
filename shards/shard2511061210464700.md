1. Regular numbers([['numero' is 'number' in Italian|'numeri']])
	1. Ones and Teens
		- [['un', 'uno', 'una' is 'one' in Italian]]
		- [['due' is 'two' in Italian]]
		- [['Tre' is 'three' in Italian]]
		- [['quattro' is 'four' in Italian]]
		- [['cinque' is 'five' in Italian]]
		- [['Sei' is 'six' in Italian]]
		- [['Sette' is 'seven' in Italian]]
		- [['Otto' is 8 in Italian]]
		- [['Nove' is 9 in Italian]]
		- [['Dieci' is 10 in Italian]]
		- [['undici' is 11 in Italian]]
		- [['Dodici' is 12 in Italian]]
		- [['tredici' is 13 in Italian]]
		- [['quattordici' is 14 in Italian]]
	2. Tens
		- [['venti' is 20 in Italian]]
		- [['trenta' is 30 in Italian]]
		- [['quaranta' is 'forty' in Italian]]
		- [['cinquanta' is 'fifty' in Italian]]
		- [['settanta' is 'seventy' in Italian]]
		- [['novanta' is 90 in Italian]]
			- [[In Italian, numbers in the ones place are simply attached(sometimes with some alteration) to those in the tens place, like English, as in 'forty-five' becomes 'quarantacinque']]
				- Other examples (slight spelling alteration): [['trentun' is 31 in Italian|'trentun = 31']], [['ventotto' is 28 in Italian|'ventotto = 28']]
				- (no spelling alteration): [['ventinove' is 29 in Italian|'ventinove = 29']]
	3. Hundreds
		- [['trecento' is 300 in Italian]]
			- For expressing hundreds that has a tens place: [[Italian express the hundreds as it does the tens, by setting the tens after the hundred]]
				- Example: [['trecentosessantacinque' is 365 in Italian]]
	4. Thousands
		- [['mille' is 'thousand' in Italian]]
			- [[In Italian, When saying '{number} mille'({num} thousand), 'mille' becomes 'mila']]
	5. Millions
		- [[Expressing 'millione' ('million') in Italian is similar to the way it is said in English except that the plural is used when expressing numbers exceeding a million]]
