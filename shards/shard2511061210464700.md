1. Regular numbers
	1. Ones
		- [['due' is 'two' in Italian]]
		- [['Tre' is 'three' in Italian]]
		- [['quattro' is 'four' in Italian]]
		- [['cinque' is 'five' in Italian]]
		- [['Sei' is 'six' in Italian]]
		- [['Sette' is 'seven' in Italian]]
		- [['Otto' is 8 in Italian]]
		- [['Nove' is 9 in Italian]]
		- [['Dieci' is 10 in Italian]]
	2. Tens
		- [['quaranta' is 'forty' in Italian]]
		- [['cinquanta' is 'fifty' in Italian]]
		- [['settanta' is 'seventy' in Italian]]
			- [[In Italian, numbers in the ones place are simply attached to those in the tens place, like English, as in 'forty-five' becomes 'quarantacinque']]
	3. Thousands
		- [['mille' is 'thousand' in Italian]]
			- [[In Italian, When saying '{number} mille'({num} thousand), 'mille' becomes 'mila']]
	4. Millions
		- [[Expressing 'millione' ('million') in Italian is similar to the way it is said in English except that the plural is used when expressing numbers exceeding a million]]
