[[A verb that is conjugated irregularly in the present does not mean it is conjugated irregularly in the past in Italian]]

1. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)vo' in Italian verbs indicates the first person past tense]]
2. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)vi' in Italian verbs indicates the second person past tense]]
3. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)va' in Italian verbs indicates the third person singular past tense]]
4. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)vamo' in Italian verbs indicates the first person plural past tense]]
5. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)vate' in Italian verbs indicates the second person plural past tense]]
6. [[The ending '(-a,e,i)vano' in Italian verbs indicates the third person plural past tense]]
