[['A' means 'at' in Italian|'a']] can be used in Italian to say 'to whom'

It is a way of expressing [[Italian Indirect Objects|indirect objects]]

Example:
'Mi piace'(it pleases me) -> 'a [[In Italian, before a preposition, 'me' and 'te' are used instead of 'mi' and 'ti'|me]] piace'(it pleases to me)
