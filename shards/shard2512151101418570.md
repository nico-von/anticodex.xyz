The root for the verb [['veste' is 'he, she, or it dresses' in Italian|'veste']] is a [[Verb conjugations to indicate the doer of the action in Italian|regular verb]] in Italian

And it is 'vestire' because [[In Italian, the regular '-ire' verb form when conjugated to show that the action is done by a plural second person, 'voi', its 'to {verb}' ending is replaced with '-ite'|its 'voi' form is 'vestite' that ends in '-ite']]

Therefore, the conjugations of 'vestire' are:
1. 'vesto'
2. 'vesti'
3. 'veste'
4. 'vestiamo'
5. 'vestite'
6. 'vestono'
