Formal
Pronouns:
1. [['Lei' is 'you'(formal) in Italian]]
2. [['essi' is the pronoun 'they' in Italian]]
3. [['esse' is a synonym of 'loro' in Italian]]
4. [['egli' is another word for the pronoun 'he' in Italian]]
5. [['essa' is another singular third person feminine pronoun in Italian]]
6. [['ella' is 'she'(formal) in Italian]]
7. [['{article} qual... -e, -i' is  'who' or 'that', like 'che' in Italian but is used when you want the gender stated to prevent ambiguity]]

Formal indirect Objects
1. [['Le' is the indirect object 'ti'(meaning 'to, from you', formal) in Italian]]

Possessive
2. [['Suo'(capital S) is the formal form of 'tuo' in Italian]]
3. [['Suoi' is the possessive 'your'(plural formal) in Italian]]

Syntax:
1. [[The third person singular conjugation is used for addressing someone formally in Italian]]


Informal
1. [['ciao' can be used when greeting friends in Italian]]
2. [['si +{third person singular conjugation}' can also be used informally to mean 'we {verb}'(first person plural) like '{verb}...-iamo' in Italian]]

Informal [[About the Italian imperative|Imperative]]:
1. [['Non {to verb}' is used as the informal negative second person imperative]]
