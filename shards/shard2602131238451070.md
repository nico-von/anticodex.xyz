The problem of [[In a prisoner's dillema, to 'discourage' defection players are subject to punishment, and to 'encourage' cooperation, players are subject to reward|rewards to encourage cooperation]] in a [[A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate|prisoner's dillema]] is that they cannot be given in advance, and promised rewards may not be trustworthy

[[Rewards for encouraging cooperation in prisoner's dillema are made more credible by making promises mutual and enforced by third parties that will benefit from the cooperation]]
