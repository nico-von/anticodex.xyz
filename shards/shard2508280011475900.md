'Mi' is 'myself' in Italian. 

Also, [['mi' is the indirect object meaning 'to, from me' in Italian]]
### Excerpt
![[eleftheriouIntroductionItalianignorecopyrightexcerptindex#^z1t30]]

Like [['ti' is the direct object 'you' in Italian|'ti']] and [['It' in Italian is 'lo'|'lo']], 'mi' could as well be placed [[Direct object pronouns in Italian usually come before the conjugated verb that points to them|before a conjugated verb as a direct pronoun]] or [[If a verb is in its Italian 'to {verb}' form, direct object pronouns can be attached to the end of it|attached to a verb if the verb is in its 'to {verb}' form]]. [[The placement of direct object pronouns in Italian are subject to these rules|These placement rules]] apply to it too.
