In Italian, when telling time with minutes, the closest hour to the current time is used, and then the minute (or if it falls on a half, [['mezzo' is 'half' in Italian|'mezzo']] or if it falls on a quarter that is not a half, [['un quarto' is 'one-fourth' in Italian|'un quarto']], for convenience) relative to it is mentioned as added(using [[A weaker and closed 'e' sound in Italian means 'and'|'e']]) or subtracted(using [['meno' is 'less' in Italian|'meno']])
