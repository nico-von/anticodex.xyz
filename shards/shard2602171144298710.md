[[The 'Tit for tat' strategy is to cooperate first and then to copy what others have done to you in the previous period|'Tit for tat']] has been tried against many other strategies in the early 1980s and was proved to be the best strategy for playing the [[A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate|prisoner's dillema]]

However, [[That even a small chance of error or misperception can cause 'tit for tat' to collapse entirely making it not ideal for the real world as, real world interactions inevitably involve mistakes|it is not ideal for the real world]]
