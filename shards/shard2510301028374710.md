Regular [['-ire' is another 'to {verb}' form ending|'-ire']] and [[Like '-are', '-ere' indicates the 'to {verb}' form of verb too.|'-ere']] Italian verbs can be [[Verb conjugations to indicate the doer of the action in Italian|conjugated]] to indicate that the doer is a third person plural 'they' by changing their [[The 'to {verb}' verb form in Italian is indicated by '-are', '-ere' or '-ire' at the verb's ending|'to {verb}' form ending]] to '-ono'. This ending is [[In Italian, the verb ending for indicating that the doer is the third person plural in Italian '-ono' and '-ano' is not usually stressed|not usually stressed]]

Of course, [[Not every Italian 'to {verb}' form ending can be altered by a single letter alone to mark the subject who does the action. Others are irregular.|irregular verbs]] can violate this, for example, the '-ere' ending of [['Sapere' is 'to know'(information) in Italian|'sapere']] in this form becomes replaced with '-ano', [['Sanno' is 'they know' in Italian|'sanno']]
