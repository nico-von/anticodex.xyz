In using a [[About decision trees|decision tree]] when playing turn-based games, you must [[The proper way of using a decision tree is not to follow the best looking branch, but to begin at an end and reason backward|not only trace back from your desired end]], but also [[That the first rule of strategy is to Anticipate the future moves of other players and then move according to the best path|anticipate what the other players’ desired ends are]]. 

However, there is a condition for a game to be **fully solvable** by [[Backward reasoning in game theory|backward reasoning]]: [[A pre-requisite for a game to be fully solvable by backward reasoning is that there must not be uncertainty of any kind]]
