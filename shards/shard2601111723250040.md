A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate(to get the best absolute [[A 'payoff' in game theory is simply the value a player will get after an interaction|payoff]]) ([[In a 'prisoner's dilemma', both players have access to a dominant strategy, which leads to a guaranteed but lower 'payoff' than if the players cooperate for a bigger payoff. However, unlike 'the dominant strategy', this outcome is not guaranteed]])
