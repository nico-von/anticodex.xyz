A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate
### Excerpt
![[dixitArtStrategyGame2010ignorecopyrightexcerptindex#^ujgg4]]

In a prisoner's dillema, players [[A 'simultaneous interaction' of decisions simply means that the decisions of players are made at the same time, and each player is aware of the existence of other players|interact simultaneously]], this means that [[Neither player has information of their opponent's current move in games where interaction is simultaneous, instead they must think about what the other is currently thinking|the players do not know of the current move of the other player as they are separately moving at the same time]]
