That the first rule of strategy is to [[When making decisions, the aims of others must be considered. If conflicting, anticipate it, if coinciding, utilise cooperation|anticipate]] the future moves of [[Players in a game of strategy are not exclusively defined as separate people, players can mean one person against himself|other players]] and then [[Backward reasoning in game theory|work backwards]] from there, following the best path towards your starting point

This means it involves prediction of what is likely to do. This involves [[When thinking strategically, the perspective of other players must always be considered(anticipated) - their information, their motivation(motives), what they think of you, whether they're irrational. As you are playing against people, not machines|putting yourself in another's perspective]]. However, [[What is meant by putting yourself in the perspective of another player is thinking what the other player is likely to do, given what they know and do not know, and their motives. It is not thinking what you will be doing if you were them.]]
