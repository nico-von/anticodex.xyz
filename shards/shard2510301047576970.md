Regular [[Most English verbs with noun forms ending in '-ation' can be transformed to their corresponding Italian 'to' verb form simply by replacing their '-ation' suffix with '-are'|'-are']] Italian verbs can be [[Verb conjugations to indicate the doer of the action in Italian|conjugations]] to indicate that the doer is a third person plural 'they' by changing their [[The 'to {verb}' verb form in Italian is indicated by '-are', '-ere' or '-ire' at the verb's ending|'to {verb}' form ending]] to '-ano'

This ending is [[In Italian, the verb ending for indicating that the doer is the third person plural in Italian '-ono' and '-ano' is not usually stressed|not usually stressed]]
