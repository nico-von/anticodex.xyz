Definite Article "The":
1. [['Il' is Italian for 'the' (for singular masculine words)]]
	1. When the word starts with a vowel: the form '[[In italian, if a word that follows 'il' starts with a vowel, they are contracted into one word, the 'i' from 'il' is removed, and an apostrophe is added after the remaining 'l'|l'{vowel-}]]' is used.
	2. For when the words begins with specific letters: 
		- Cases where [['lo' is 'the' in Italian|'lo']] must be used instead of 'il':
			1. [['lo' is used as the article 'the' in Italian for singular masculine words that begins with 's-'|when the singular masculine word start with the letter 's']]
			2. [['lo' is used as the article 'the' in Italian for singular masculine words that begins with 'z-']]
	3. Plural Forms
		1. [[The plural of 'il' is 'i' (not used on all plural words, used only on masculine plural words that would not be used with 'gli')]]
		2. [[When 'gli' is used|'Gli' is the article 'the' for plural words that are masculine and start with vowels or special consonants]]
