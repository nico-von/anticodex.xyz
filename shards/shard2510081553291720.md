In Italian, some words that precede [[An open 'e' sound, spelled 'è', in Italian means 'third person..is'|'è']] can be contracted with it to form a single word 
1. [[The Italian phrase 'Quando è che' can be contracted to just 'Quandèche']]
2. [[Like 'quando' 'cosa' can as well be combined with 'è che' and contracted to 'cosè che']]
3. [[In Italian, when 'è' comes after 'come', they are contracted to 'comè']]
4. [[The Italian word 'Dove' can be contracted with 'è', becoming 'dovè', to say 'Where is it(or he, or she)' all by itself]]
5. [[When 'ci' is used as 'there' in Italian, and is followed by 'è', the two words contract together becoming 'c'è']]
6. [['cioè' is 'that is' in Italian]]
7. [[In Italian, when 'ne' is followed by 'è', they contract into one word forming 'n'è']]

### Common words
'[['Il' is Italian for 'the' (for singular masculine words)|il]]':
	1. [[In italian, if a word that follows 'il' starts with a vowel, they are contracted into one word, the 'i' from 'il' is removed, and an apostrophe is added after the remaining 'l']]

'[['It' in Italian is 'lo'|lo]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'lo' is followed by 'avere', they are contracted, the 'o' in 'lo' is replaced with an apostrophe]]

'[['da' is 'from' in Italian|da]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'da' is followed by 'il', they contract into one word forming 'dal']]
	2. [[In Italian, when 'da' is followed by 'gli', they contract into one word forming 'dagli']]
	3. [[In Italian, when 'da' is followed by 'le', they contract into one word forming 'dalle']]
	4. [[In Italian, when 'da' is followed by 'l'', they contract into one word forming 'dall'']]
	5. [[In Italian, when 'da' is followed by 'la', they contract into one word forming 'dalla']]
	
'[['a' is the locational, directional, 'to' in Italian|a]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'a' precedes 'il', they are contracted together to form 'al']]
	2. [[In Italian, 'A' and 'le' when used together in succession, are contracted into one word 'Alle']]
	3. [[In Italian, when 'a' is followed by 'gli', they contract into one word forming 'agli']]
	4. [[In Italian, when 'a' is followed by 'le', they contract into one word forming 'alle']]
	5. [[In Italian, when 'a' is followed by 'l'', they contract into one word forming 'all'']]
	6. [[In Italian, when 'a' is followed by 'la', they contract into one word forming 'alla']]

'[['la' is Italian for 'the' (for singular feminine words)|la]]':
	1. [[In Italian, if a vowel follows 'la', it contracts with the word it follows, and the 'a' in 'la' is replaced by an apostrophe]] 
	2. [[In Italian, when 'la' is followed by 'avere', they are contracted, the 'a' in 'la' is replaced with an apostrophe]]

'[['di' is the Italian for 'of' or 'from'|di]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'di' follows the article 'il' or 'la', they contract together forming 'del' or 'della']], [[In Italian, when 'di' precedes 'l'' they contract into one word forming 'dell'']]
	2. [[In Italian, if 'di' precedes 'le', they are contracted together to 'delle']]
	3. [[In Italian, when 'di' and 'i' are used together, they contract into 'dei']]
	4. [[In Italian, if 'di' precedes certain words like 'Italia', it contracts with it, the 'i' of 'di' becoming an apostrophe]]
	5. [[In Italian, if 'di' follows 'gli', they contract into one word, 'degli']]
	6. [[In Italian, when 'di' is followed by 'lo', they contract into one word forming 'dello']]

'[[Since 'a thing' is 'una cosa' in Italian, and 'cosa' is feminine, 'una' stands for the feminine article 'a'|una]]':
	1. [[In Italian, 'una' when followed by a vowel, contracts with the following word, the 'a' of 'una' becoming an apostrophe]]
	
'[['in' is also 'in' in Italian|in]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'il', they contract into one word forming 'nel']]
	2. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'la' they contract into 'nella']]
	3. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'i', they contract into one word forming 'nei']]
	4. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'le', they contract into one word forming 'nelle']]
	5. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'l'', they contract into one word forming 'nell'']]
	6. [[In Italian, when 'in' is followed by 'gli', they contract into one word forming 'negli']]
	7. [[When 'in' is followed by 'lo', they contract into one word forming 'nello']]


'[['Su' is 'on'(above, up) in Italian|su]]':
	1. [[In Italian, when 'su' is followed by 'i', they contract into one word forming 'sui']]
	2. [[In Italian, when 'su' is followed by 'gli', they contract into one word forming 'sugli']]
	3. [[In Italian, when 'su' is followed by 'la', they contract into one word forming 'sulla']]
	
'[['Con' means 'with' in Italian|col]]': 
	1. [[In Italian, when 'con' is followed by 'il', they contract into one word forming 'col']]
	
[[List of numbers in Italian|numbers]]:
	1. [[In Italian, some words that begin with a vowel can be attached to the end of numbers by dropping the ending vowel of the number]] 
		- Examples: [['trenta' is 30 in Italian|'trenta']] + [['un', 'uno', 'una' is 1 in Italian|'un']]=[['trentun' is 31 in Italian|trentun]], [['ventiquattr'ore' is 'twenty-four hours' in Italian|ventiquattr'ore]]
