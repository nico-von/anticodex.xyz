Article for an indefinite quantity:
1. [[In Italian, 'di' is used with a definite article to express an indefinite quantity of plural nouns]]

Indefinite article for a quantity of one, commonly in English as "a":
1. [[Since 'a thing' is 'una cosa' in Italian, and 'cosa' is feminine, 'una' stands for the feminine article 'a'|'una' - feminine indefinite article]]
	- Ommitted on special cases: [[In Italian, the article for 'a' is dropped when saying that a person has a spouse]]
