In a [[A prisoner's dilemma occurs when opposing players, acting rationally out of self-interest and fear of betrayal, choose to act against each other, even though both parties would be better off if they cooperate|prisoner's dillema]], to 'discourage' defection ([[The outcome in a dominant strategy is that the player always gets the relative best payoff(guaranteed) of each situation and not the absolute best payoff(i.e. The highest reward)|reason being it not a guarantee of the absolute best payoff]]) players are subject to punishment, and to 'encourage' [[Players in a 'prisoner's dilemma' can either 'defect' or 'cooperate'|cooperation]]([[In a 'prisoner's dillema' to 'defect' is the dominant strategy for both players, however, if both 'defect' they will do worse than they would if they both 'cooperate' instead|for both to get the best payoff]]), players are subject to reward
