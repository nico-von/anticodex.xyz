'Tutta' is the [[About feminine Italian words|feminine form]] of [['Tutto' is 'everything' in Italian|'Tutto'(meaning 'everything')]]

### Excerpt
![[eleftheriouIntroductionItalianignorecopyrightexcerptindex#^0aykz]]

It can be used to say 'all of' or 'whole' such as the pattern [[The pattern 'tutti(-e) e {numero}' means 'all of the {number}' in Italian|'tutti(-e) e {numero}' means 'all of the {number}']]. Another example is 'Tutta [['La' is Italian for 'the' (for singular feminine words)|la]] [['casa' is 'house' in Italian|casa]]': 'the whole house'
