People [[Experiments showed, that when players(in a prisoner's dillema) are allowed to punish cheating players but suffer a personal cost in exchange, a significant amount of punishments are still executed despite the cost|engage in the act of punishing 'social cheaters' despite it being a dominated strategy(as it doesn't give them any personal gains, and costs them something in exchange)]] because it provides them a psychological benefit or pleasure. This was observed in the brains of the players during the experiment: the part of the brain involved in pleasure lights up(specifically the 'dorsal striatum') during the act of executing punishment
